Objectives of the Action

A digital framework for multi-lateral collaboration

Between 1500 and 1800, a revolution in postal communication allowed ordinary men and women to scatter letters across and beyond Europe. This exchange helped knit together what contemporaries called the respublica litteraria, a knowledge-based civil society, crucial to that era’s intellectual breakthroughs, and formative of many modern European values and institutions.

Ironically, the exchange of letters which created this community also dispersed the documentation required to study it, posing enormous difficulties for historians of the subject ever since.

To reassemble that scattered material and chart the history of that imagined community we need a revolution in digital communications.

This COST Action is dedicated to envisaging the open-access, open-source, transnational digital infrastructure capable of facilitating the radically multilateral collaboration needed to reassemble this scattered documentation and to support a new generation of scholarly methods and research questions.

Geographical Extent

A Network of Researchers

COST Action IS1310 Reassembling the Republic of Letters, 1500–1800 is therefore essentially a networking programme. It emerged in response to the expectation that that the ongoing revolution in digital communications technology can solve the scholarly problem created by the evolution of postal communication in the early modern period, thanks to the contribution of various sholars and researchers spread in different research groups.

Research at National Level

Spread all over the coutries involved in the COST Action there are a number of reaserachers dealing with the digital humanities, and especially with the study of correspondence.

Fostering Collaboration

Thanks to a particular form of grant called STSM (Short Term Scientific Missions) people had been able of meet with the aim of lead very specific projects.
In the network, nodes are representing COST-involved nations. Edges are directional, meaning one or more people travelled from a country to a hosting one, while the size is representative of the sum of in-going and out-going connection every nation assists.
As it is possible to deduce from the image, not all the nations have been involved (as a hosting one or not) in an STSM.

In essence, a new scholarly network is needed in order to study the older scholarly network of the early modern period. To sustain this new network, we need a new breed of digital networking tools; and the purpose of this Action is to assemble the network needed to design these tools: a network to devise a networking platform to support a network studying past networks.

COST Action does not found research itself, but provide the environment for adding up to something greater than the sum of individual parts.

FOUR COMMUNITIES

In order to uderstand how to outline those digital tools described before, it is better, if not necessary, to gather a wide amount of specific knowledges which are nowadays scattered across a number of different skills of various field of study: since there is data to be managed, some technical capability has to be emplyed, since interfaces have to be designed and built, some method has to be called in action. The understanding of humanistic matter is nonetheless fundamental.

The challenge of this particular programme is then to coordinate them all in order to obtain the best collaboration results as possible.
